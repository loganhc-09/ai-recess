# AI Recess voice

The brand voice for everything AI Recess ships publicly: site copy, recap pages, social captions, video scripts, Discord announcements. Distilled from transcripts of the real prep calls and live episodes (March through June 2026), not invented. Load this before writing any AI Recess outbound copy. The weekly recap pipeline carries a compact version in `tools/recap/digest-prompt.md`.

Kevin's personal byline uses his own voice DNA doc, not this one. This is the three-creator group voice.

## The voice in one paragraph

Three friends at recess who happen to be deep in AI, talking to people they refuse to make feel stupid. Playful on the surface, receipts underneath. Every piece of hype comes with its wart in the same breath. The reader is a peer on the playground, never a lead in a funnel. When it gets serious, the sentences just get shorter; the register never goes corporate.

## Core stance

- **Real value, said plainly.** "We're trying to create real value. And that requires discussion and debate." The offer is stated in one sentence with a date or a number, then the copy moves on. No urgency theater.
- **Learning together, not lecturing.** "This is not just us coming down from the mountaintop with two tablets of information. This is also learning from each other." The community's ideas get named and celebrated; the creators are participants, not gurus.
- **Nobody feels stupid.** "We define our terms real quick so that nobody feels stupid." Every acronym gets translated on first use. Asking the dumb question is sanctioned behavior; the hosts do it to each other on air.
- **No hype without a wart.** The canonical shape: "it's a little buggy, it's a little slow, it's a little weird, but what it is..." Praise for a tool is not credible here unless the flaw rides along.
- **Anti-noise.** "I don't listen to that noise. Because I'm trying to actually build things that are valuable and useful." Neither doomer nor cheerleader survives unchallenged; the house register for AI news is "a little bit of levity slash existential dread."
- **Not behind, right here.** The grade-school ladder exists to orient, never to rank. "The goal is the right grade for the task, not the top grade." Recess, not a leaderboard.

## Lexicon (current terms)

Use these; they are load-bearing brand assets:

- **recessors** — members. Also "the community." Never "users," "subscribers," or "audience."
- **the playground** — the Discord and the community space. "Submit it to the playground."
- **goody bag** — the weekly resource drop. Current official term; "weekly drops" is acceptable in casual copy.
- **show and tell** — members demoing what they built.
- **What's Popping** — the news segment.
- **shallow dive / deep dive** — their own depth labels for content.
- **kiddie pool / deep end** — beginner-safe versus advanced territory.
- **baby steps / hopscotch steps** — pacing metaphors for adoption.
- **grades / "What grade are you in?"** — the adoption ladder. Pair with reassurance: all grades welcome, you're not behind.
- **rules of the game** — defining terms before playing.
- **think print** — a person's cognitive fingerprint (V's coinage).
- **claw** — a member's own Claude, affectionately. "My claw called me out."
- **AI slop / the noise** — what we are against.
- **That's recess.** — the soft CTA. "Want the rest? That's recess."

Legacy terms that must never appear in current copy: Skool or "School," Friday calls or Friday sessions, any price other than $20/month, "waitlist." The live is TikTok Live, Tuesdays 9pm ET. Members join through the site.

## The three voices

The group voice is a volley: one person starts a bit, the others extend it rather than top it, and someone names the delight out loud ("Oh, it's so good. It's fun. It's fun."). Written copy should feel like the transcript of that volley cleaned up, not like one narrator.

### Kevin

Short declarative bursts chained with "So..." and "and... and..." when excited, fragments for punctuation: "So." "Sweet." "One shot it." Explains by concrete analogy, then mechanism, then what it means for you. Cost and practicality framing ("that cost me $0"). Dry understatement after wild stories. Self-aware about obsession: "I was up at 4:30 a.m. this morning, I couldn't sleep thinking about this." Hedges every hype: "I'll be honest..." Receipt-sharing reflex: "I'll find it and send it." Boundary-first on safety: "Everyone thinks the first thing you do is add your Gmail to your agent. It's like, no. It's probably the last thing you do."

### Logan

Longest turns, theatrical setup and payoff, deliberate detail, comic timing through self-interruption. Lowercase in chat, "tbh," "mwahahaha." Ships-first: "let's start with just... something and improve it from there." Roasts the post, protects the person. Pop culture as punchline (Seinfeld, Schitt's Creek). Marketing vocabulary made casual. Trailing softeners: "type of thing," "or something like that." Doubled words when agreeing: "Cool, cool, cool." Signature energy: "I'm legitimately so geekily excited."

### V

Layered sentences scaffolded with "essentially" and "sort of," thinking out loud on purpose: "And I'm. I'm still processing." Extended food and world analogies that land a technical point (the hawker-stall menu as an agent's table of contents; the gym that only works if you show up). Absurdist idea collisions. Adds a lens instead of negating: "I see it from, like, three different lenses." Checks transfer at the end: "So that you get it." Rapid-fire one-word delight: "Done." "Stop it." "I'm here for it."

## Hard rules

1. No em dashes. Commas, periods, or restructure.
2. Never "vibe coding." Say AI engineering, builds, ships. (It slipped into one old episode; it is banned now.)
3. No member names, member quotes, or member-identifying detail in public copy. Creators may be named. Inside the Discord, first names are fine.
4. No doxxing energy anywhere, even in roasts. Punch at the post, not the person.
5. No rage bait, no engagement-gimmick voting, no scarcity theater. A business ask is one sentence: what it is, what it costs, when.
6. No LinkedIn-speak. "What X taught me about B2B SaaS" is the house definition of cringe. If a line would survive on LinkedIn unedited, rewrite it.
7. No unexplained jargon. First use gets a plain-words translation, in-line, without slowing down.
8. No hype without a wart. If everything about a tool sounds great, the copy is lying by omission.
9. Imperfection stays in. "We failed at keeping this portion light. Sorry." is on-brand; polish that erases the humans is not.
10. Numbers are receipts, not flexes. Counts of things shared and built, yes. Internal metrics, membership counts, revenue, never.
11. Nobody gets framed negatively, creator or member, even accurately, even as a joke. No "threatening," "fighting," "calling out," or drama framing in recaps, emails, or site copy. A heated debate is reported by what it produced (the rule, the takeaway, the open question), not by who clashed. Copy must always make the playground look like a place you would want to walk into. Recess: "Three of us tried the same agent setup and got wildly different bills. The checklist that fixed it is in the Discord. That's recess."

Slop: "Don't miss out! Join today for exclusive access to premium resources!"
Recess: "Members already have all seven links. $20 a month, cancel whenever."

Recess: "We define our terms real quick so that nobody feels stupid. Then we get into it."
